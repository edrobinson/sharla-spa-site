The Author

Elara Vance spent her youth translating the sound of rain into ink on scrap paper.

MIDNIGHT ECHOES

The moon is a thin silver coin
Tossed into the well of the sky.
We wait for the splash,
But only hear the stars
Whispering our names back to us.

THE SALT GARDEN

We planted regret in rows,
Watered with the tide.
Nothing grew but the white crust
Of things we meant to say
Before the ocean claimed the land.
